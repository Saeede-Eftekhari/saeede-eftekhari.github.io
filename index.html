Research

My research is organized around two primary streams in the Information Systems domain.

Health Information Exchanges

The first stream examines the impact of Health Information Exchanges (HIEs) on clinical practices, healthcare access, physicians’ strategic behavior, and patient decision-making. This research combines causal econometric modeling and social network analysis using large-scale longitudinal healthcare datasets, as well as qualitative study.

Social Media

The second stream explores how social media communication reflects and shapes societal disruptions, including online mobilization and mental well-being during prolonged crises. This work draws on computational social science, machine learning, text analytics, and longitudinal social media data to study evolving digital behaviors and online communities.
